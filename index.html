Question 1 (12 × 1 = 12)

Attempt all questions. Each carries 1 mark.

Note: Questions are based on prose & poetry lessons from Gul Mohar Class 7. Vocabulary items included.

  1. In "The Scholarship Jacket", who raised Martha?
    • Her parents
    • Her grandparents
    • Her aunt and uncle
    • School hostel
    Answer: b) Her grandparents
    Explanation: Martha was raised by her grandparents after her parents could not support her / passed away.
  2. What colour is the scholarship jacket?
    • Red and blue
    • Gold and green
    • Black and white
    • Purple and silver
    Answer: b) Gold and green
    Explanation: The jacket is described as gold with a big gold 'S' and name in gold letters (often with green elements in descriptions).
  3. In "My Unknown Friend", where does the narrator meet the friendly stranger?
    • In a park
    • On a train
    • At school
    • In a market
    Answer: b) On a train
    Explanation: The humorous mistaken-identity incident occurs in a train compartment.
  4. What nickname did the Germans give to the brave women pilots in "Night Witches"?
    • Sky Queens
    • Night Witches
    • Star Warriors
    • Moon Angels
    Answer: b) Night Witches
    Explanation: The Germans called the 588th Night Bomber Regiment "Nachthexen" (Night Witches) due to their stealthy night raids.
  5. Choose the synonym of "valedictorian":
    • Topper / highest grader
    • Athlete
    • Artist
    • Musician
    Answer: a) Topper / highest grader
    Explanation: Valedictorian refers to the student with the highest academic rank who delivers the farewell address.
  6. Antonym of "brave" (from Night Witches context):
    • Courageous
    • Fearful
    • Strong
    • Smart
    Answer: b) Fearful
    Explanation: Brave × fearful / cowardly.

Section B — Prose

Question 2

“I had been a straight A student since first grade... My oldest sister, Rosie, had won the jacket a few years back, and I fully expected to also... The scholarship jacket had a big gold S on the left front side and your name written in gold letters on the pocket.”
  1. Who is the speaker / narrator? (Recall)
    Answer: Martha
    Explanation: The story is narrated in the first person by Martha.
  2. What is special about the scholarship jacket? (Understanding)
    Answer: It is awarded annually to the student with the highest grades; it has a big gold 'S' and the winner's name in gold letters on the pocket.
    Explanation: It symbolizes academic excellence and is a source of great pride.
  3. Why was Martha so confident she would win it? (Recall + Understanding)
    Answer: She had been a straight-A student since first grade, and her older sister Rosie had previously won it.
    Explanation: Her consistent academic performance and family history gave her confidence.
  4. What injustice did Martha face later in the story? (Critical Analysis)
    Answer: The principal told her she would only receive the jacket if her grandfather paid $15, even though she had earned it through grades.
    Explanation: The school bent the rules to favor a richer student, showing discrimination based on race and money.
  5. If you were Martha’s grandfather, would you have paid the $15? Why / why not? (Application)
    Sample Answer: No, I would not pay — because paying would mean accepting and supporting an unfair, discriminatory rule.
    Explanation: The jacket should be awarded purely on merit; paying rewards injustice. (Accept reasoned personal responses.)
  6. What lesson does the story teach about hard work and fairness? (Critical Analysis)
    Answer: Hard work deserves fair recognition regardless of race, money, or background; one should stand up against injustice with dignity.
    Explanation: The story highlights discrimination and the importance of integrity and self-respect.

Question 3

“The stranger entered the compartment and at once began talking to me as though we had known each other for years... ‘Old man,’ he said warmly, ‘it’s good to see you again!’ I was completely puzzled.”
  1. Where is this event happening? (Recall)
    Answer: In a train compartment
    Explanation: The meeting takes place during a train journey.
  2. How does the stranger behave? (Understanding)
    Answer: He behaves very familiarly and warmly, as if they are old friends.
    Explanation: He greets the narrator enthusiastically and talks freely.
  3. Why is the narrator confused? (Understanding)
    Answer: Because he has never met the stranger before.
    Explanation: The stranger acts like they know each other, but the narrator does not recognize him at all.
  4. What makes this meeting interesting / funny? (Critical Analysis)
    Answer: The mistaken identity and the stranger's extreme confidence create humorous awkwardness.
    Explanation: The contrast between the stranger's familiarity and the narrator's confusion is comical.
  5. Have you ever met someone who acted like they knew you? Share briefly. (Application)
    Sample Answer: Yes, once someone mistook me for their cousin at a wedding and talked for 10 minutes before realizing the mistake.
    Explanation: Personal experience (accept any reasonable short answer).
  6. What does the story show about human behaviour and friendliness? (Critical Analysis)
    Answer: Some people are naturally outgoing and assume familiarity easily; friendliness can sometimes lead to funny misunderstandings.
    Explanation: The story celebrates warmth while showing how assumptions can create humor.

Question 4

“They flew old, slow wooden planes at night... Their engines were so quiet that the only sound was the wind through the struts—like witches on broomsticks. That is how the Germans named them—Night Witches.”
  1. Who are the "Night Witches"? (Recall)
    Answer: The women pilots of the Soviet 588th Night Bomber Regiment.
    Explanation: All-female unit in WWII known for night bombing missions.
  2. Why were their planes difficult to detect? (Understanding)
    Answer: They flew old, slow, wooden planes with quiet engines at night.
    Explanation: The planes made almost no engine noise; only wind sound was heard.
  3. Why did the Germans fear them? (Critical Analysis)
    Answer: Because of their stealthy, accurate night attacks that were hard to defend against.
    Explanation: Their surprise raids caused significant damage and psychological fear.
  4. What qualities of the pilots impress you most? (Critical Analysis)
    Answer: Courage, determination, skill, and bravery in dangerous conditions with inferior equipment.
    Explanation: Flying at night in old planes under enemy fire shows exceptional heroism.
  5. If you could ask one Night Witch a question, what would it be? (Application)
    Sample Answer: “What gave you the strength to keep flying night after night despite the danger?”
    Explanation: Personal response (accept thoughtful questions).
  6. What does their story tell us about courage? (Critical Analysis)
    Answer: True courage is shown by ordinary people (especially women in this case) doing extraordinary things in the face of fear and hardship.
    Explanation: They defied stereotypes and risked their lives for their country.

Question 5

“A sea of foliage girds our garden-ground,
In which, like a shipwrecked pearl, the moon lies drowned...”
  1. What does the poet compare the garden to? (Recall)
    Answer: A sea (of foliage)
    Explanation: The garden is surrounded by a “sea of foliage”.
  2. Why is “sea of foliage” a suitable image? (Understanding)
    Answer: It suggests vastness, movement, and depth — like waves of leaves surrounding the garden.
    Explanation: Creates a sense of being enclosed in a green, flowing, ocean-like space.
  3. How does this picture make you feel? (Critical Analysis)
    Sample Answer: Calm, peaceful, and enchanted — like being in a magical, quiet world.
    Explanation: Personal response (accept calm, serene, dreamy feelings).
  4. Describe your favourite natural place using one simile. (Application)
    Sample Answer: My favourite hill station looks like a green carpet spread under the sky.
    Explanation: Accept any creative, correct simile.
  5. What other beautiful image do you find in the lines? (Recall)
    Answer: The moon is compared to a “shipwrecked pearl” drowned in the foliage.
    Explanation: Vivid, delicate, and romantic image of the moon.
  6. What mood does the poem create? (Critical Analysis)
    Answer: Peaceful, dreamy, serene, and slightly mysterious.
    Explanation: The imagery evokes tranquility and wonder.

Question 6

“O woodman, spare that tree!
Touch not a single bough!
In youth it sheltered me,
And I’ll protect it now...”
  1. Who is requesting the woodcutter? (Recall)
    Answer: The poet / speaker
    Explanation: The speaker pleads with the woodman.
  2. Why does the speaker want to save the tree? (Understanding)
    Answer: It sheltered him in his youth / childhood.
    Explanation: The tree holds sentimental value and memories.
  3. What services does a tree provide us? (Critical Analysis)
    Answer: Shade, oxygen, fruits, wood, prevents soil erosion, supports wildlife, cools the air, etc.
    Explanation: Trees are essential for life and environment.
  4. What small step can you take to save trees? (Application)
    Sample Answer: Plant one tree every year / use paper bags instead of plastic / avoid wasting paper.
    Explanation: Accept practical, realistic steps.
  5. Which line shows deep emotion? (Recall)
    Answer: “In youth it sheltered me, / And I’ll protect it now...”
    Explanation: Shows gratitude and determination.
  6. How is this poem relevant today? (Critical Analysis)
    Answer: It reminds us to protect trees and nature in the face of deforestation and climate change.
    Explanation: Environmental message is more urgent now than ever.

Question 7

“Rise, brothers, rise; the wakening skies pray to the morning light,
The wind lies asleep in the arms of the dawn like a child that has cried all night...”
  1. Who is being called to rise? (Recall)
    Answer: Brothers (fishermen / comrades / people)
    Explanation: The poet calls his fellow fishermen / brothers to wake up.
  2. What time of day is shown in the poem? (Understanding)
    Answer: Early morning / dawn
    Explanation: “Wakening skies”, “morning light”, “dawn” indicate sunrise.
  3. What emotion does the poet express? (Critical Analysis)
    Answer: Enthusiasm, hope, energy, and encouragement.
    Explanation: The call to rise is full of vitality and optimism.
  4. Write 2-3 lines encouraging someone to start a new journey. (Application)
    Sample Answer: Rise, friend, rise! A new day awaits with open arms. Step forward with courage — your dreams are calling you now.
    Explanation: Accept any positive, motivational lines.
  5. Which comparison of the wind do you like most? (Recall)
    Answer: “The wind lies asleep in the arms of the dawn like a child that has cried all night”
    Explanation: Gentle, tender, and vivid personification.
  6. What does the sea represent in this poem? (Critical Analysis)
    Answer: Livelihood, adventure, challenge, and opportunity.
    Explanation: For fishermen, the sea is both provider and place of hard work.
